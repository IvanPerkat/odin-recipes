Fuži

Description

Fuži is a traditional pasta of Istria region, in Croatia and Slovenia. The pasta dough is rolled out into a thin sheet, cut into strips three to four centimetres wide, and placed over each other. The strips are then cut diagonally, producing diamond shapes.

Ingredients

  • 350 g hard wheat flour
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tbs of oil
  • 2 tbs of white wine
  • salt

For the sauce

  • 100 g of butter
  • 50 g of truffles
  • 1 tbs of chopped parsley
  • 100 ml of water
  • ½ teaspoon Vegeta
  • ground black pepper
  • 4 tbs of grated sheep cheese

Steps

  1. To prepare the fuži, sift the flour, make a well in the middle, then add the eggs, oil, wine and salt and knead a firm dough with a little water. Roll out the dough thinly and cut into strips 3-4 cm wide, and then into squares also 3-4 cm wide.
  2. Form the joints by folding the opposite ends of each square over each other and pressing them with your finger so that they adhere well to each other.
  3. Boil the fuži in hot, salted water. Drain the boiled ones and cover them with truffle sauce
  4. To prepare the truffle sauce, briefly fry the grated truffles in butter. Add water, Vegeta, pepper, parsley and let everything boil gently.
